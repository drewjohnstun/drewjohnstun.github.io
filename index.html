the-multi-agent-prompt.txt Needs: ChatGPT Pro · Ultra ON 
You are my Work Manager. I'm handing you my entire to-do list.
Do NOT do this as one chat. Split it into parallel sub-agents and run them at once.

Spin up FOUR agents, each owning its lane:
- RESEARCH  → facts, competitors, sources (use web access)
- ANALYTICS → data, numbers, patterns, takeaways
- STRATEGY  → plans, calendars, decks, positioning
- CONTENT   → all writing: posts, captions, descriptions

If there are more tasks than agents, some agents own two. That's fine — run all four in parallel.

Rules for every agent:
1. State which task you took and which agent you are before you start.
2. Do the actual work — real deliverables, not a plan to do the work.
3. Use web access for anything past your knowledge cutoff.
4. Flag anything you're unsure about instead of guessing.
5. End with "confidence: high / medium / low — why."

When all four finish, give me one summary: what's done, what needs my eyes, what's next.

CONTEXT:
[Who you are. What you're working on. Who it's for. 2-3 sentences is plenty.]

MY TO-DO LIST:

1. [RESEARCH]  ...
2. [ANALYTICS] ...
3. [STRATEGY]  ...
4. [STRATEGY]  ...
5. [CONTENT]   ...
6. [CONTENT]   ...

Tag each task with the lane that should own it. Be specific about what "done"
looks like — "find 10 competitors and rank them by X" beats "research competitors."

The second free thing

Which tier to run it on

GPT-5.6 ships as three tiers at very different prices. Most people leave everything on the flagship and overpay for it.

Pick in five seconds

Running agents on real work — a to-do list, a project, anything multi-step → Sol. It's the only tier with Ultra mode, and Ultra is the only way to run agents in parallel at all.

Chatting, writing, thinking out loud → Terra. Roughly last year's flagship quality at half of Sol's price. Don't pay Sol rates to draft an email.

Bulk and simple — tagging, sorting, lookups → Luna. Cheapest and fastest. Save it for work that doesn't need to think hard.

Three rules that save you money

  1. Match the tier to the job, not to the day. Agents on Sol, chat on Terra, bulk on Luna — switch per task. Leaving everything on Sol is how most people overpay.
  2. Turn on web access. Knowledge stops at February 2026. Without the web it's blind to everything since, and it will still answer confidently.
  3. Judge it like a manager. It's a fast junior, not a finished employee. Budget one editing pass — a couple of tasks in six will need your eyes.

One warning nobody puts on the box

Ultra burns your usage about four times faster, because it's four agents. On the $20 plan you will hit the wall fast. Ultra needs Pro.
